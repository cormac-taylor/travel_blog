My Travel Destinations

Lisbon, Portugal

Lisbon is a city that hums with charm and history. With its cobblestone streets, terracotta rooftops, and breathtaking ocean views, Portugal’s capital is as enchanting as it is welcoming. Walking through Lisbon felt like exploring a series of neighborhoods, each with its own personality and treasures. One minute I was mesmerized by intricate tilework on old buildings, and the next, I found myself overlooking the sea from a hillside viewpoint.

  1. Alfama District
  2. Belém Tower
  3. LX Factory

Cape Town, South Africa

Cape Town is a city where mountains meet the ocean and urban life blends effortlessly with natural beauty. The landscape alone had me captivated from the moment I arrived. Whether I was hiking to the top of Table Mountain or strolling along the beach, Cape Town’s outdoors were awe-inspiring. But it’s not just the scenery; the city’s vibrant culture, rich history, and welcoming people left a lasting impression.

  1. Table Mountain
  2. Bo-Kaap
  3. Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden

Kyoto, Japan

Kyoto offers a serene escape into Japan’s past, where every corner is filled with temples, tea houses, and beautiful gardens. Unlike the buzz of Tokyo, Kyoto felt tranquil and contemplative. Walking through its neighborhoods felt like traveling back in time, where traditional wooden architecture and quiet pathways were a refreshing change of pace.

  1. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove
  2. Fushimi Inari Shrine
  3. Nijo Castle
